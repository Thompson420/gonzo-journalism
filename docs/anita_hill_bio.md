# Anita Hill - Testimony That Shocked America

## Full Biography

**Anita Faye Hill** was born on July 30, 1954, in Lone Oak, Oklahoma. She is an American law professor and attorney who became a national figure during the 1991 Supreme Court confirmation hearings for Clarence Thomas, where she testified about sexual harassment.

## Early Life

- Born in Lone Oak, Oklahoma
- Youngest of 9 children
- Family: Farm workers, church community
- Graduated from Yale Law School (1980)

## Career

### Law Professor
- Taught at University of Oklahoma (1977-1986)
- Yale Law School (1981-1985)
- Brandeis University (current)

### Legal Work
- Attorney at Wassington, D.C. law firm
- EEOC (Equal Employment Opportunity Commission)
- Published on gender discrimination

## The Clarence Thomas Hearings

### Background
- 1991: Clarence Thomas nominated to Supreme Court
- Anita Hill testified about sexual harassment
- Thomas denied all allegations

### Her Testimony
- Described repeated sexual harassment
- Called him "a man I have great respect for"
- Detailed accounts over months

### Impact
- "The single most watched event in television history at that time"
- Changed national conversation on sexual harassment
- Inspired women to come forward

## Aftermath

### Personal Cost
- Death threats
- Character assassination
- "Trailblazer for survivors everywhere"

### Cultural Impact
- "YouTube moment" before YouTube
- Inspired Generation X/Y feminist movement
- Helped pass Violence Against Women Act (1994)

### Recognition
- Received 2021 Kennedy Center Honors
- Inducted into National Women's Hall of Fame
- Multiple honorary degrees

## Legacy

> "I have been asked whether I would do it again. And I have to say: I would do it again because I think it's important to tell the truth."

### Impact on #MeToo
- Precursor to 2017 movement
- Taught survivors they could speak
- Normalized believing women

- **Secondary**: C-SPAN archives, National Women's Hall of Fame
