# Anna Politkovskaya - The Voice of Chechnya

## Full Biography

**Anna Sergeyevna Politkovskaya** was born on August 30, 1958, in New York City to Russian parents and died on October 7, 2006, in Moscow. She was a Russian journalist, author, and human rights activist known for her fearless reporting on the Chechen wars and criticism of the Russian government.

## Early Life

- Born in New York while her parents were studying at Columbia University
- Family returned to Moscow when she was 6 years old
- Father: Russian journalist Vladimir Politkovsky (killed in 1999)
- Graduated from Moscow State University (journalism)

## Career

### Early Career (1980s-1990s)
- Worked for Soviet and Russian newspapers
- First major work: covering Soviet war in Afghanistan (1980s)
- Reported on Boris Yeltsin's reforms

### Chechen Wars (1994-2000s)
- Became world-famous for reporting on the Chechen wars
- Documented:
  - War crimes by Russian military
  - Kidnappings and disappearances
  - Torture in Russian prisons
  - Corruption in Russian government
- Her reports were published in **Novaya Gazeta** and international media

### Key Investigations

1. **Budennovsk Hospital Siege (1995)**
   - Held hostage in hospital with patients
   - Negotiated with Chechen militants

2. **Beslan School Siege (2004)**
   - Reported from inside the crisis
   - Criticized government response

3. **Chechen Atrocities**
   - Documented mass graves
   - Interviewed victims and witnesses
   - Exposed systematic torture

## Writings

### Books
- **"A Small Corner of Hell: Dispatches from Chechnya" (2003)** - eyewitness accounts
- **"Putin's Russia" (2004)** - critique of Putin's regime
- **"Life and Death in the Siege" (2005)** - Beslan coverage

### Novaya Gazeta
- Worked for Russia's last independent newspaper
- 60 Minutes, BBC, Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty

## Awards & Recognition

- **2002** - International Women's Media Foundation award
- **2003** - UNESCO/Guillermo Cano World Press Freedom Prize
- **2004** - European Parliament's Sakharov Prize (nominee)
- **2007** - National Press Club award (posthumous)

## Death

- **October 7, 2006** - Shot dead in Moscow elevator
- Shot 3 times in head
- Killer escaped
- One of the most high-profile journalist murders in Russian history

### Aftermath
- Three men convicted in 2014
- suspected mastermind never arrested
- Investigation ongoing

## Legacy

- Symbol of press freedom
- "Martyr for journalism"
- Her work still referenced for Chechen war coverage
- Anna Politkovskaya Award for Courage in Journalism established

## Archive Status

- **Primary**: Novaya Gazeta archives
- **Secondary**: Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International
- **Books**: Published in 20+ languages
