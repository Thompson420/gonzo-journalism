# Daphne Caruana Galizia: The One-Woman WikiLeaks

> "There are crooks everywhere you look now. The situation is desperate."

---

## The Legend

Daphne Caruana Galizia (1964-2017) was Malta's most fearless investigative journalist — and one of the most dangerous women in the world to be.

For three decades, she exposed corruption at the highest levels of the Maltese government until she was assassinated by a car bomb in 2017.

---

## Her Story

### Early Career (1987-2008)
- Started as a newspaper reporter in 1987
- Wrote about corruption, nepotism, and political scandals
- Faced constant legal threats and intimidation

### The Blog Era (2008-2017)
In 2008, she launched "Running Commentary" — a personal blog that became Malta's most-read website.

**What she exposed:**
- Panama Papers (2016) — Revealed that Malta's Prime Minister's chief of staff and Tourism Minister had secret offshore companies
- Egrant scandal — Alleged that the Prime Minister's wife owned a secret company
- 17 Black — Exposed a Dubai-based company linked to government corruption
- Killing of journalist Daphne Caruana Galizia — her own assassination investigation

### The Price
- Faced 48 libel lawsuits
- House firebombed twice
- Dog killed
- Death threats constantly
- Arrested for "breaking political silence"

---

## The Assassination

**October 16, 2017:**
- Car bomb exploded her Peugeot 108 near her home in Bidnija
- Body parts found 80 meters from the blast
- Her son Matthew found her remains

**The aftermath:**
- Three men arrested (brothers George and Alfred Degiorgio, and Vince Muscat)
- 2019: Businessman Yorgen Fenech arrested on his yacht
- Alleged to be the mastermind
- 2021: Vince Muscat sentenced to 15 years
- 2022: Degiorgio brothers sentenced to 40 years

---

## Legacy

- **The Daphne Project**: 45 journalists from 18 countries continued her work
- **European Parliament press room** renamed in her honor
- **Daphne Caruana Galizia Prize for Journalism** established by EU Parliament
- **Her last words**: "There are crooks everywhere you look now. The situation is desperate."
