# Daphne Caruana Galizia - Malta's Martyr

## Full Biography

**Daphne Anne Caruana Galizia** was born on August 9, 1964, in Sliema, Malta, and died on October 16, 2017. She was a Maltese journalist, anti-corruption activist, and blogger who was assassinated in one of the most notorious murders in European Union history.

## Early Life

- Born in Sliema, Malta
- Father: Form 5 education, shipyard worker
- Attended St. Paul's School
- Studied in the UK (secretarial college)

## Career

### Early Work (1980s-1990s)
- Started writing for The Times of Malta
- Shift work to raise family

### The Running Commentary (2008-2017)
- Started her blog: **Running Commentary**
- Became Malta's most prominent investigative journalist
- Focused on:
  - Political corruption
  - Money laundering
  - Links between politicians and organized crime

### Key Investigations

1. **Pilatus Bank Scandal**
   - Exposed money laundering
   - Linked prime minister's wife to offshore accounts
   - "Pilatus Bank was a bank in name only"

2. **Egrant Inquiry**
   - Exposed: PM Joseph Muscat's wife owned Egrant Inc
   - Panama Papers connection
   - Led to protests

3. **Stewart Healthcare**
   - Hospital contract fraud
   - Linked to government officials

4. **SiGAME**
   - Government slot machine company
   - Missing €3 billion

## Awards

- **2017** - International Press Institute World Press Freedom Hero
- **2017** - Transparency International Anti-Corruption Award (posthumous)
- **2018** - UNESCO/Guillermo Cano Prize (posthumous)

## Threats and Intimidation

- Faced 50+ lawsuits from politicians
- Constant police protection denied
- "48 libel cases in her final year alone"

## Death

### October 16, 2017
- Car bomb exploded in her car
- Near her home in Bidnija, Malta
- Killed instantly
- Body parts found 200 meters away

### International Response
- Massive protests in Malta
- EU demands answers
- "Journalist murdered in EU territory"

### Aftermath

- Three men convicted (2021)
- Mastermind: Vince Muscat (2022)
- Ongoing investigations
- PM Muscat resigned (2019)

## Legacy

- Symbol of press freedom in Europe
- "One-woman WikiLeaks"
- Inspired Maltese diaspora journalists
- Daphne Caruana Galizia Prize for Journalism established
