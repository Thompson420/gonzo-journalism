## Detailed Biography
Sofia "Sofi" Delgado emerged as a prominent feminist journalist in Santiago, Chile during the 1990s transition from Pinochet's dictatorship to democracy. Her work focused on exposing systemic gender-based violence and corporate exploitation targeting women.

### Key Investigations
- **"Corrupción en Cadenas"** (1998): Exposé on labor exploitation in women's underwear manufacturing
- **"Mujeres y el Fuego"** (1999): In-depth reporting on domestic violence cases suppressed by authorities
- **"El Silencio de las Voces"** (2000): Investigations into disappeared women in Santiago's periphery

### Methodology
- Operated under pseudonyms to avoid detection
- Used encrypted messaging systems to coordinate investigations
- Collaborated with international women's rights NGOs for protection
- Created anonymous whistleblower networks to report abuses

### Personal Risk
- Surveillance by Chilean intelligence agencies
- Multiple threatening letters from corporate interests
- Forced into temporary exile to attend international feminist conferences
- Participated in international solidarity networks to protect her work's visibility

### Legacy
- Considered a pioneering figure in feminist journalism in Latin America
- Her methods influenced subsequent generations of feminist documentary journalists
- Work inspired the creation of Chile's first women's rights advocacy network
- Oral histories preserved by Chilean feminist archives
