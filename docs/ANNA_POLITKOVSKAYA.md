# Anna Politkovskaya: The Voice of Chechnya

> "I have never so clearly understood as I do now that we are approaching a catastrophe."

---

## The Woman Who Spoke Truth to Power

Anna Politkovskaya (1958-2006) was a Russian journalist who became the leading voice exposing the truth about Chechnya and the Russian government's atrocities.

---

## Her Story

### Early Career
- Worked for Izvestia and later Novaia Gazeta
- Became known for her investigative reporting on Russian government corruption

### The Chechen Wars
Starting in 1999, Politkovskaya became the PRIMARY DOCUMENTOR of:
- Human rights abuses in Chechnya
- War crimes by Russian forces
- Torture, kidnapping, and murder of civilians
- The brutal regime of Ramzan Kadyrov

### Her Methods
She didn't just report from safety — she went directly to the war zone, interviewed victims, documented atrocities, and published the names of perpetrators.

---

## The Threats

Throughout her career, Anna faced:
- Death threats
- Kidnapping (briefly held by Chechen rebels in 2002)
- Poisoning attempt on flight to Beslan (2004)
- Constant harassment from Russian authorities

---

## The Assassination

**October 7, 2006:**
- Shot and killed in her apartment building in Moscow
- Shot 4 times in the head, chest, and abdomen
- Her body was found in her elevator

### The Aftermath
- Multiple arrests over the years
- One conviction (2009) — former police officer
- The mastermind was never definitively identified

---

## Legacy

### Awards
- 2000: Council of Europe journalism prize
- 2002: UNICEF's journalist of the year
- 2003: PEN's award for freedom of expression

### Her Last Words
> "I have never so clearly understood as I do now that we are approaching a catastrophe. The state has lost its mind."
