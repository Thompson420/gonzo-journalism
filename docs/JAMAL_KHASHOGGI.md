# Jamal Khashoggi: The Icon of Press Freedom

> "I was starting to suspect that I could be a target... But I kept writing."

---

## The Man

Jamal Khashoggi (1958-2018) was a Saudi journalist, Washington Post columnist, and founder of Democracy for the Arab World Now (DAWN). He was assassinated in the Saudi consulate in Istanbul on October 2, 2018.

---

## His Story

### Early Career
- Worked as a journalist for various Saudi publications
- Close to the Saudi royal family in the 1980s-1990s
- Eventually became critical of the government

### The Exile
- Left Saudi Arabia in 2017
- Became a Washington Post columnist
- Founded DAWN (Democracy for the Arab World Now)
- Criticized Saudi Arabia's war in Yemen and Crown Prince MBS

### What He Exposed
- Saudi Arabia's Yemen war
- Human rights abuses
- Corruption in the Saudi government
- The crackdown on dissidents

---

## The Assassination

### October 2, 2018
- Entered the Saudi consulate in Istanbul to get marriage documents
- Never left
- Body was dismembered
- Bones were found weeks later

### The Aftermath
- CIA concluded Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman ordered the killing
- International outrage
- Time Person of the Year 2018 ("The Guardians and War Correspondents")

---

## Legacy

Jamal Khashoggi became a SYMBOL of:
- The danger facing journalists
- Press freedom under attack
- The cost of speaking truth to power
