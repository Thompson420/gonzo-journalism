# Latin America: The Most Dangerous Region

> More journalists are killed in Latin America than anywhere else in the world.

---

## Overview

Latin America — particularly Mexico, Brazil, and Central America — is the most dangerous region for journalists in the democratic world. Drug cartels, paramilitary groups, and corrupt governments create a perfect storm of violence against the press.

To be a gonzo journalist here is to risk your life daily.

---

## Mexico: The Cartel Wars

### 🇲🇽 Miroslava Breach (1962-2017)

**The Voice of Chihuahua**

**The Story:**
- 30-year career exposing drug cartel infiltration in Chihuahua politics
- Exposed Governor César Duarte's $900 million embezzlement
- Investigated human rights violations in Sierra Tarahumara
- Documented female homicides in Ciudad Juárez

**The End:**
March 23, 2017 — Shot 8 times in her car. Assassins linked to Sinaloa Cartel.

**Legacy:** Her murder is still not fully resolved. Her sister continues fighting for justice.

## The Argentine Connection

### 🇦🇷 Rodolfo Walsh (1927-1977)

**The Father of Investigative Journalism**

**The Story:**
- Argentine writer and journalist
- "Operación Masacre" (1957) — considered the FIRST non-fiction novel
- Exposed 1956 military repression in Argentina
- Founded investigative journalism in Latin America

**The End:**
- Disappeared during Dirty War (1977)
- Believed killed by military dictatorship
- His daughter Maria Victoria also disappeared

**Legacy:** Considered the father of Latin American investigative journalism. His work "Operación Masacre" predates Truman Capote's "In Cold Blood" by 9 years.
