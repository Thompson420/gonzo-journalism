# Jason Rezaian: 544 Days in an Iranian Prison

> "What I was convicted of was not espionage — it was journalism."

---

## The Journalist

Jason Rezaian is an Iranian-American journalist who served as the Washington Post's Tehran bureau chief. He was imprisoned in Iran for 544 days.

---

## His Story

### Early Career
- Born in California to Iranian parents
- Grew up between US and Iran
- Became Washington Post's Tehran bureau chief in 2012

### What He Did
- Covered Iranian politics, society, and culture
- Wrote about everyday life in Iran
- One of the few Western journalists working in Tehran

---

## The Arrest

### July 2014
- Arrested at his home in Tehran
- Held in Evin Prison (notorious for political prisoners)
- Held in solitary confinement for months

### The Charges
- Convicted of "espionage"
- Convicted of "collaborating with hostile governments"
- Sentenced to prison

### The Conditions
- 544 DAYS in prison
- Solitary confinement
- Limited contact with family
- Poor medical care

---

## The Release

### January 2016
- Released in a prisoner swap
- Freed along with other Iranian-Americans
- Allowed to leave Iran

### Aftermath
- Returned to the US
- Wrote book: "Prisoner: My 544 Days in an Iranian Prison"
- Continues to advocate for journalist prisoners

---

## Legacy

Jason Rezaian's case became a SYMBOL of:
- Iran's crackdown on press freedom
- The dangers facing foreign journalists
- The importance of continuing to report despite persecution
