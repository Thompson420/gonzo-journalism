# Maria Ressa - Nobel Laureate

## Full Biography

**Maria Angelita Ressa** was born on October 3, 1963, in Davao City, Philippines. She is a Filipino-American journalist and author who co-founded **Rappler**, one of the Philippines' leading digital news platforms. In 2021, she became the first Filipino to win the **Nobel Peace Prize**.

## Early Life

- Born in Davao City, Philippines
- Father: Filipino journalist (died when she was young)
- Mother: Filipino-American
- Moved to the U.S. at age 9
- Princeton University (BA)
- Columbia University (MA in journalism)

## Career

### CNN Period (1993-2012)
- Started as a field producer in Manila
- Became CNN's Manila bureau chief
- Covered major Asian stories:
  - Asian financial crisis (1997)
  - Jemaah Islamiyah terrorist network
  - Warlord politics in the Philippines

### ABS-CBN (2012-2016)
- Joined Philippine network as news executive
- Network shut down by government in 2020

### Rappler (2012-Present)
- Co-founded with colleagues
- Pioneered "social media journalism"
- First Filipino digital-only news organization
- Known for:
  - Fact-checking
  - Data journalism
  - Accountability reporting

## Key Investigations

### 1. "The Baguio City Mayor's Son" (2012)
- Investigated illegal logging
- First major Rappler story

### 2. "The Drug War" (2016-2020)
- Documented extrajudicial killings
- Named victims in "The War on Drugs" series
- Exposed police orchestrating murders

### 3. "Smartmatic" (2019)
- Exposed cheating in 2019 midterm elections
- Automated election manipulation

### 4. Facebook/Digital Threats
- Documented massive disinformation campaigns
- Trol farms targeting journalists
- Social media manipulation

## Repression and Legal Attacks

### Arrests and Convictions
- **2020**: Arrested for cyberlibel
- **2022**: Convicted (cyberlibel)
- Multiple arrest warrants
- Constant legal harassment

### Personal Threats
- Death threats
- Online harassment campaigns
- "Fdashboard" - coordinated attack on Facebook
- Pistol-whipped by police (2019)

## Nobel Peace Prize 2021

### Citation
> "For their struggle to safeguard freedom of expression, which is a precondition for democracy and lasting peace."

### Historic
- First Filipino Nobel Peace Prize winner
- First Southeast Asian woman to win
- First journalist to win since 1935 (Carl von Ossietzky)

### Award Speech
- Spoke about "weaponization of social media"
- Called for "truth over lies"
- Thanked Filipino journalists

## Awards and Recognition

- **2021**: Nobel Peace Prize (with Dmitry Muratov)
- **2018**: UNESCO/Guillermo Cano Press Freedom Prize
- **2018**: Time Person of the Year (with other journalists)
- **2019**: Michael Richards Prize for Courage
- **2022**: Goldman Environmental Prize
