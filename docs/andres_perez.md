## Detailed Biography
Andrés "Andy" Pérez emerged as a key figure in Mexico's student movement journalism during the turbulent 1970s. His guerrilla-style reporting combined direct participation with surrealist storytelling techniques.

### Key Publications
- **"Rabia del Alma"** (1972): Chapbook documenting Tlatelolco protests
- **"Carta desde el Infierno"** (1973): Underground newspaper manuscript
- **"Ojo de Lagartija"** (1974): Poetry collection recording social unrest

### Methodology
- Participated directly in 1971 student protests
- Used surrealist techniques to describe violence
- Self-published underground works with steganography
- Maintained contacts with international journalists for smuggled information

### Personal Risk
- Arrested multiple times during protests
- Forced to flee to Chicago (1975)
- Work banned under PRI regime
- Died in exile under mysterious circumstances (1992)

### Legacy
- Considered "voice of Mexican student movement"
- Inspired "Andrew movement" youth journalism collective
- Work studied in University of Mexico's academic program
- Digital archives maintained by Mexican Journalist Union
