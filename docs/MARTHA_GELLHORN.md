# Martha Gellhorn

## Overview
Martha Gellhorn was one of the most celebrated war correspondents of the 20th century, known for her immersive frontline reporting.

## Biography
- **Born:** November 8, 1908, St. Louis, Missouri
- **Died:** October 15, 1998, London
- **Nationality:** American

## Career
Gellhorn covered virtually every major conflict from the Spanish Civil War through the Gulf War. She was married to Ernest Hemingway.

## Notable Works
- "A Variety of Convictions" (1970)
- "The Face of War" (1988)
- "Travels with My Aunt" (1969)

## Gonzo Connection
Gellhorn's first-person war reporting predated and influenced the gonzo movement, setting the standard for immersive conflict journalism.

## Legacy
Widely regarded as one of the greatest war correspondents of all time.
