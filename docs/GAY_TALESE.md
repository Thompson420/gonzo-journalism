# Gay Talese

## Overview
Gay Talese is an American journalist and writer known for his contributions to New Journalism.

## Biography
- **Born:** February 11, 1932, New York City
- **Nationality:** American

## Career
Talese worked for The New York Times and Esquire magazine, writing in-depth profile pieces and narrative journalism.

## Notable Works
- "The Loneliest Boy" (1966)
- "Thy Neighbor's Wife" (1980)
- "The Bridge" (2004)

## Gonzo Connection
Talese's immersive reporting style, often spending months with his subjects, embodied the gonzo approach to journalism.

## Legacy
Considered one of the pioneers of New Journalism alongside Wolfe, Didion, and Thompson.
