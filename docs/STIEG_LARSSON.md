# Stieg Larsson: The Millennium Legend

> He died before his books were published. Then they became a worldwide phenomenon.

---

## The Man Behind the Books

Stieg Larsson (1954-2004) was a Swedish journalist and writer best known for the "Millennium" trilogy, including "The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo."

---

## His Story

### Early Life
- Born in 1954 in Sweden
- Grew up in a family with strong political beliefs

### His Work as a Journalist
- Worked as an investigative journalist
- Specialized in exposing right-wing extremist groups in Sweden
- Documented the connections between neo-Nazi organizations

### The Research
For decades, Stieg researched:
- Swedish right-wing extremist groups
- The connections between extremist organizations
- The rise of Nazism in Sweden

---

## The Millennium Trilogy

### What He Wrote
1. **The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo** (2005)
2. **The Girl Who Played with Fire** (2006)
3. **The Girl Who Kicked the Hornets' Nest** (2007)

### The Main Characters
- **Mikael Blomkvist** — Investigative journalist
- **Lisbeth Salander** — Hacker and investigator

### What the Books Exposed
- Corporate corruption
- Domestic violence
- Nazi history in Sweden
- Government conspiracies

---

## His Death

### 2004
- Died of a heart attack
- He was only 50 years old
- Died before any of his books were published

### The Aftermath
- His partner, Eva Gabrielsson, fought to get his books published
- The trilogy became a worldwide phenomenon
- Sold over 100 million copies
- Adapted into successful films

---

## Legacy

Stieg Larsson's work became a SYMBOL of:
- The power of investigative journalism
- Telling stories that expose the truth
- The impact one person can have on the world
