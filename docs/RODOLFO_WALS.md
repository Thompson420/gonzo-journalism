# Rodolfo Walsh: The Argentine Pioneer

> He wrote the first non-fiction novel—9 years before Capote.

---

## The Journalist

Rodolfo Walsh (1938-1977) was an Argentine journalist and writer considered the father of investigative journalism in Latin America.

---

## His Story

### Early Career
- Started as a journalist in the 1950s
- Worked for various publications

### The Book
- "Operación Masacre" (1957)
- Investigative book about extrajudicial killings
- First non-fiction novel in Spanish
- Predates Truman Capote's "In Cold Blood" by 9 years

### What He Did
- Exposed police brutality
- Named perpetrators
- Documented human rights abuses

---

## The Price

### 1977
- Disappeared during Argentina's Dirty War
- Believed killed by military dictatorship
- Never seen again

---

## Legacy

Rodolfo Walsh represents:
- The birth of investigative journalism in Latin America
- Courage in the face of oppression
- The power of truth-telling
