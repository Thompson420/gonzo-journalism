# Louis Theroux - The Master of Immersion

## Full Biography

**Louis Theroux** was born on July 20, 1960, in Singapore. He is a British documentary filmmaker and author known for his immersive, Gonzo-style documentaries. Son of James Theroux and nephew of travel writer Paul Theroux.

## Early Life

- Born in Singapore (British family)
- Moved to the UK at age 2
- Educated at Westminster School
- Oxford University: Philosophy, Politics and Economics (PPE)

## Career

### Early Career
- Worked for Andy Warhol's Interview magazine
- Wrote for magazines
- BBC documentary filmmaker

### Breakthrough: "Weird Weekends" (1998-2000)
- First major documentary series
- Immersive approach to subcultures
- Became his signature style

## Documentary Series

### 1. Weird Weekends (1998-2000)
- Explored unusual subcultures:
  - UFO believers
  - Porn stars
  - LA gangs
  - Right-wing militias

### 2. When Louis Theroux... (2001-2018)
- Reconnected with subjects from Weird Weekends
- Longer-form investigations

### 3. BBC2/Mega Collection
- Miami mega jail
- The most unknown places
- Undercover in North Korea
- Alcoholics Anonymous

### 4. Distilled (2024)
- New series on Americana

## Gonzo Methodology

### Techniques
1. **First-person narration** - Unlike typical documentaries
2. **Embedded immersion** - Spends weeks with subjects
3. **Conversational intimacy** - Befriends subjects
4. **Ambiguous endings** - Doesn't judge
5. **Dark humor** - Witty observations

### What Makes Him Gonzo
- Subjective perspective
- Personal reactions included
- Becomes part of story
- Uncomfortable questions
- "Naïve" questioning
