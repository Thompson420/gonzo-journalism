# Truman Capote

## Overview
Truman Capote was an American writer whose innovative approach to nonfiction storytelling revolutionized journalism.

## Biography
- **Born:** September 30, 1924, New Orleans, Louisiana
- **Died:** August 25, 1984, Los Angeles, California
- **Nationality:** American

## Career
Capote's work spanned short stories, novels, plays, and nonfiction. His carefully crafted prose style influenced generations of writers.

## Notable Works
- "Breakfast at Tiffany's" (1958)
- "In Cold Blood" (1966) - non-fiction novel
- "Other Voices, Other Rooms" (1948)

## Gonzo Connection
"In Cold Blood" pioneered the "non-fiction novel" form, blending journalistic reporting with literary narrative techniques.

## Legacy
Capote's work helped establish literary journalism as a distinct art form.
