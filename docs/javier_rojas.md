## Detailed Biography
Javier "Che" Rojas emerged as a key figure in Uruguay's underground resistance during the military dictatorship era (1973-1985). His work as both cartoonist and zine publisher made him a target of the regime.

### Key Publications
- **"Grito Libre"** (1982-1985): Monthly zine featuring political cartoons, satire, and underground news
- **"El Tintero"** (1984): Special edition focusing on censorship and press freedom
- **"Caricatura de Resistencia"** (1983): Collection of political cartoons smuggled out of Uruguay

### Methodology
- Used pseudonyms to avoid detection ("Che" was his underground alias)
- Worked with network of underground printers and distributors
- Smuggled materials to neighboring countries for publication
- Collaborated with Chilean and Argentine underground press networks

### Personal Risk
- Multiple arrests for "subversive activities"
- Forced into exile in Argentina (1985)
- Continued publishing from exile until return to Uruguay (1989)
- Maintained underground contacts despite exile status

### Legacy
- Considered pioneer of political cartooning in Latin America
- His zine inspired similar publications in other dictatorships
- Work documented in "Zines of the Americas" academic study
- Influenced contemporary Latin American political cartoonists
