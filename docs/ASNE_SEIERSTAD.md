# Asne Seierstad: The Norwegian Storyteller

> She goes to the heart of the story and stays until she understands it.

---

## The Journalist

Åsne Seierstad is one of Norway's most famous journalists and authors, known for her immersive, book-length investigations of major events.

---

## Her Story

### Background
- Norwegian journalist and author
- Studied at University of Oslo
- Became known for her in-depth investigations

### Her Method
- Goes TO the story
- Stays for MONTHS or YEARS
- Builds relationships with subjects
- Writes immersive, book-length narratives

---

## Major Works

### "The Bookseller of Kabul" (2003)
- Went undercover in Afghanistan
- Lived with a bookseller and his family
- Documented life under Taliban rule
- Became an international bestseller

### "One of Us" (2015)
- Investigated the Anders Breivik massacre in Norway
- 2011: Breivik killed 77 people
- Spent years with victims' families
- The massacre happened in her home country

---

## What Makes Her Gonzo

- IMMERSIVE: She doesn't just observe — she embeds
- PERSONAL: She forms deep relationships with subjects
- LONG-FORM: Books, not articles
- DANGEROUS: She goes to war zones and dangerous places

---

## Awards

- Multiple Norwegian literary awards
- International recognition for her journalism

---

## Legacy

Åsne Seierstad represents:
- The power of immersive long-form journalism
- Going deep, not just wide
- Understanding a story from the inside
