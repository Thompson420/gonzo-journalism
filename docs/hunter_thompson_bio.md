# Hunter S. Thompson - The Father of Gonzo

## Full Biography

**Hunter Stockton Thompson** was born on November 18, 1937, in Louisville, Kentucky, and died on February 20, 2005. He was an American journalist and author who pioneered the style of journalism known as "gonzo" - a first-person, immersive approach that abandoned traditional objectivity.

## Early Life

- Born in Louisville, Kentucky, to father Jack Thompson (sheriff's deputy) and mother Virginia (civil rights activist)
- Dropped out of high school at 15
- Worked as a journalist for local newspapers in the 1950s
- Brief stint in the U.S. Air Force (discharged with mental health diagnosis)

## Career Highlights

### Early Work (1960s)
- Wrote for magazines like **Scanlan's Monthly** and **The New York Times**
- His 1965 article "The Kentucky Derby Is Decadent and Depraved" is considered proto-gonzo
- First book: **"Hell's Angels" (1966)** - embedded with the motorcycle gang for 18 months

### The Gonzo Period (1970s)
- **"Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas" (1971)** - defining gonzo text
- Published in **Rolling Stone** magazine
- Covered the 1972 presidential campaign, the Watergate scandal
- Famous for his " Raoul Duke" persona

### Later Career
- Ran for sheriff of Pitkin County, Colorado (1970)
- Founded the **Gonzo journalism** movement
- Wrote "The Rum Diary" (1998), later made into a film
- Final book: **"Kingdom of Fear" (2003)**

## Gonzo Methodology

### Key Principles
1. **First-person immersion** - Journalist becomes the story
2. **No objective observer** - Embrace subjectivity
3. ** literary techniques** - Fiction writing in nonfiction
4. **Participant observer** - Embed with subjects
5. **Psychedelic realism** - Heightened, surreal descriptions

### Famous Quote
> "The Edge... There is no honest way to explain it because the only people who really know where it is are the ones who have gone over."

## Personal Life

- Married three times
- Son: **Juan Thompson** (journalist)
- Known for: Wild parties, cannon firing, drug use, eccentric behavior
- Built his compound: **"Owl Farm"** in Colorado

## Death

- Died by suicide on February 20, 2005
- Final request: Firecracker cannon salute instead of funeral
- ashes fired from a cannon atop a 153-foot tower he built

## Legacy

- Created an entire journalism movement
- Influenced generations of journalists, writers, filmmakers
- "Gonzo" now in the dictionary
- Films: "Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas" (1998), "The Rum Diary" (2011), "Gonzo" (2008 documentary)

Thompson's gonzo journalism influenced:
- Film (fear and Loathing)
- Music (Springsteen, Rolling Stones referenced him)
- Literature (countless writers)

- Podcast journalism (Sarah Koenig, etc.)
