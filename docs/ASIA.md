# Asia-Pacific: Surviving Dictatorship

> From China's censorship to the Philippines' "red tagging" — Asia's journalists face unique challenges.

---

## China: The Censorship Machine

### 🇨🇳 Zhang Zhan (~1980s-present)

**The Hunger Strike Hero**

**The Story:**
- Former lawyer turned citizen journalist
- February 2020: Traveled to Wuhan to document COVID-19 pandemic
- Posted about government detentions, harassment of victims' families

**Imprisoned:**
- "Disappeared" May 2020
- Convicted of "picking quarrels and provoking trouble"
- HUNGER STRIKE in prison
- Force-fed, weight dropped to 37kg
- Released May 2024
- Re-detained August 2024
- Sentenced to 4 MORE years (September 2025)

**Current Status:** Still imprisoned (2025)

## The Philippines: "Red Tag" to Prison

### 🇵🇭 Frenchie Mae Cumpio (1999-present)

**The 21-Year-Old Political Prisoner**

**The Story:**
- Executive director of local news website (at age 20!)
- Investigated army harassment of rural farmers
- Exposed forced displacement in Eastern Visayas

**Imprisoned:**
- Arrested February 2020 (age 21)
- Weapons PLANTED on her (grenade, firearm)
- STILL IN PRISON WITHOUT TRIAL (2026) — over 6 years
- Faces 40 years if convicted

**International Support:** RSF, UN, and press freedom groups have condemned her case.

## India: The Sting Master

### 🇮🇳 Rana Ayyub (1984-present)

**The Gujarat Files**

**The Story:**
- Investigative journalist, Washington Post columnist
- Went UNDERCOVER for 10 months (2018)
- Posed as "Maithili Tyagi"
- Exposed 2002 Gujarat riots cover-up
- Got Amit Shah (Modi's close ally) jailed

**The Price:**
- Death threats
- Deepfake porn created of her
- Doxxed
- Multiple international awards

## Pakistan: Killing in Exile

### 🇵🇰 Arshad Sharif (1972-2022)

**The Story:**
- Prominent Pakistani investigative journalist
- Critical of military establishment
- Fled Pakistan after sedition charges
- Moved to Dubai, then Kenya

**The End:**
October 2022 — SHOT DEAD in Kenya
- Official story: "mistaken identity"
- Critics say targeted by Pakistani authorities

## Themes

1. **State Censorship** — China is the world's largest jailer of journalists.

2. **Political Retaliation** — Journalists in India, Pakistan, Philippines face charges like "sedition" and "terrorism."

3. **The "Red Tag" Phenomenon** — In Philippines, journalists labeled "communist" are targeted.

4. **International Networks** — ICIJ (International Consortium of Investigative Journalists) connects Asia-Pacific journalists to global investigations.
