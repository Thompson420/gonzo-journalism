# Norman Mailer

## Overview
Norman Mailer was an American novelist, journalist, and playwright who was a pioneering figure in New Journalism.

## Biography
- **Born:** January 7, 1923, Long Branch, New Jersey
- **Died:** November 10, 2007, New York City
- **Nationality:** American

## Career
Mailer's work combined literary fiction with journalism, creating a new form of immersive, first-person reporting.

## Notable Works
- "The Naked and the Dead" (1948)
- "The Executioner's Song" (1979) - won Pulitzer Prize
- "The Fight" (1975) - covering Ali vs. Frazier

## Gonzo Connection
Mailer's "The Fight" is considered a classic of sports gonzo journalism, written in the same immersive style as Thompson's work.

## Legacy
Mailer helped establish New Journalism as a major literary movement.
