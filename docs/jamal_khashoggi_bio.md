# Jamal Khashoggi - The Voice of Dissent

## Full Biography

**Jamal Ahmad Khashoggi** was born on October 13, 1958, in Medina, Saudi Arabia, and died on October 2, 2018, in the Saudi Arabian consulate in Istanbul, Turkey. He was a Saudi-American journalist, author, and columnist for The Washington Post who was assassinated in one of the most notorious murders of the modern era.

## Early Life

- Born in Medina, Saudi Arabia
- Grandfather was Muhammad Khashoggi (personal physician to King Abdulaziz)
- Family had close ties to Saudi royal family
- Studied at Indiana State University (BA) and Harvard University (MA)

## Career

### Early Work (1980s-1990s)
- Started as a news editor at Saudi Gazette
- Worked for Al Arab News
- Close to Saudi royal family, served as advisor

### Mainstream Period (2000s-2010s)
- Editor-in-chief of Al Arab News
- Spokesman for Prince Alwaleed bin Talal
- Columnist for The Washington Post (from 2017)
- Founded **DAWN** (Democracy for the Arab World) - exile news organization

### Key Work

1. **Washington Post columns (2017-2018)**
   - Criticized Saudi Arabia's war in Yemen
   - Exposed crackdowns on dissidents
   - Questioned Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman

2. **DAWN Organization**
   - Founded in 2017 to promote democracy
   - Documented human rights abuses
   - Published exile journalism

## What He Reported On

- Saudi Arabia's war in Yemen
- Crackdown on women's rights activists
- Arrests of journalists
- Corruption in Saudi royal family
- Missing Saudi blogger: Waleed Abu al-Kair

## Exile and Threats

- Left Saudi Arabia in 2017 after "wave of arrests"
- Living in exile in the United States
- Warned by Saudi friends: "You can't survive this"
- Received death threats

## The Murder

### October 2, 2018
- Entered Saudi consulate in Istanbul to get marriage documents
- Fiancée: Hatice Cengiz waited outside for 11 hours
- Never came out

### What Happened
- Murder confirmed by Saudi authorities after weeks of denial
- 15 Saudi agents involved
- Body never found (reportedly dissolved in acid)
- Crown Prince MBS implicated

### International Response
- Global outrage
- CIA concluded MBS ordered killing
- U.S. report (2021): MBS approved operation
- Sanctions imposed

## Awards & Recognition

- **Time Person of the Year 2018** (with journalists)
- **UNESCO/Guillermo Cano Prize** (posthumous 2019)
- **Right Livelihood Award** (posthumous 2019)
- **Golden Pen of Freedom** (posthumous)

## Legacy

- Symbol of journalist safety worldwide
- Sparked global conversation about journalist murders
- Inspired journalists in exile
- DAWN continues his work

## Archive Status

- **Primary**: Washington Post archives, DAWN
- **Secondary**: UN human rights investigations, Turkish police files
- **Books**: Multiple posthumous collections

> "The Arab world is being destroyed by autocrats, and the West is helping them."

> "What is important is that I raised my voice. Even if I die, others will continue."
