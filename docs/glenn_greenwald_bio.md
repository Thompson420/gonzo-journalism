# Glenn Greenwald - The Surveillance State Watchdog

## Full Biography

**Glenn Edward Greenwald** was born on March 6, 1966, in New York City. He is an American journalist, lawyer, and author known for his work on surveillance, privacy, and government accountability. He gained international prominence for revealing the Edward Snowden NSA surveillance program.

## Early Life

- Born in New York City
- Father: Investment banker
- Graduated from University of Brazil (law degree)
- Attended law school at University of Pennsylvania

## Career

### Early Legal Work
- Practiced law in New York
- Civil liberties attorney
- Focused on constitutional law

### Blogging and Early Journalism (2005-2010)
- Started salon.com blog: "Unclaimed Territory"
- Covered the NSA surveillance program early
- One of the first to write about mass surveillance

### The Guardian (2010-2013)
- Joined The Guardian US
- Broke the Edward Snowden story (June 2013)

### The Intercept (2014-2018)
- Co-founded The Intercept with Laura Poitras
- Published more Snowden documents
- Continued NSA investigations

### Independent (2018-Present)
- Left The Intercept
- Founded Substack newsletter
- Continues investigative journalism

## The Snowden Revelations

### The Story
- First met Edward Snowden in Hong Kong (June 2013)
- Published first article: "NSA collecting phone records of millions of Verizon customers"
- Exposed PRISM program
- Revealed mass surveillance of Americans

### What Was Revealed
- NSA collects metadata on virtually all phone calls
- PRISM: Direct access to Google, Facebook, Apple servers
- XKeyscore: Global internet surveillance program
- Upstream: Tapping undersea fiber optic cables

### Impact
- Global conversation on privacy
- Created "Snowden effect"
- Inspired encryption adoption
- Resigned in protest
