# Kim Wall: The Journalist Who Vanished at Sea

> She went to investigate a story. She never came home.

---

## The Journalist

Kim Wall (1987-2017) was a Swedish freelance journalist who wrote for the New York Times, the Guardian, Vice, and other publications. She was murdered in 2017.

---

## Her Story

### Background
- Swedish citizen
- Master's degree from Columbia Journalism School
- Wrote for major international publications
- Known for her investigative work

### What She Investigated
- Danish inventor Peter Madsen
- His submarine projects
- The world of Danish innovation

---

## The Last Day

### August 10, 2017
- Met Peter Madsen at his workshop in Copenhagen
- Went aboard his homemade submarine (UC3 Nautilus)
- Planned to interview him

### The Disappearance
- Madsen said he dropped her off on August 10
- She never returned
- Her phone was found

---

## The Discovery

### Days Later
- Her TORSO was found in the water
- Later, her head, arms, and legs were found separately
- She had been DECAPITATED
- Body weighted down with metal

### The Trial
- Peter Madsen tried for murder
- Claimed it was an accident
- Convicted and sentenced to life in prison

---

## Legacy

Kim Wall became a SYMBOL of:
- The dangers facing female journalists
- The risks of investigative journalism
- The need for safety protocols
