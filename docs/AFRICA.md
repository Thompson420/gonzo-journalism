# Africa: The Gonzo Underground

> In Africa, journalism isn't a profession — it's survival.

---

## Overview

Africa has some of the most dangerous countries for journalists in the world. In many places, the only way to expose corruption, crime, and human rights abuses is to go UNDERCOVER, become part of the story, and risk everything.

These aren't "gonzo" in the traditional sense — they're JOURNALISTS WHO SURVIVE.

---

## The Killers

### 🇧🇷 Tim Lopes (Brazil, 2002)

**The Story:**
- TV Globo investigative reporter
- Went into Rio favelas to investigate drug trafficking and child prostitution
- Hidden camera, undercover

**The End:**
- Kidnapped, TORTURED, EXECUTED
- Body BURNED in secret grave
- Found with 41 bone fragments

**Legacy:** His death led to increased protection for Brazilian journalists.

---

### 🇲🇽 Miroslava Breach (Mexico, 2017)

**The Story:**
- La Jornada investigative reporter based in Chihuahua
- Exposed drug cartel infiltration in politics
- Documented human rights violations

**The End:**
- Shot 8 times in her car while driving her son to school
- Cartel-linked assassins

**Legacy:** Her murder is still not fully resolved.

## The Ones Who Risk Everything

### 🇬🇭 Anas Aremeyaw Anas (Ghana)

**The Man Without a Face**

Anas is Ghana's most famous undercover journalist — and he never shows his face.

**The Story:**
- Uses disguises: madman, woman, Sheikh, anything
- Exposed 34 judges taking bribes
- "Number 12" — got footage of Ghana Football Association president taking bribes
- Exposed "galamsey" (illegal mining)

**The Price:**
- Collaborator Ahmed Hussein-Suale ASSASSINATED in 2019
- MP Kennedy Agyapong put a hit on him on live TV
- Called a "blackmailer" by a judge (defamation suit lost)

**Awards:** 20+ international awards

---

### 🇳🇬 Fisayo Soyombo (Nigeria)

**The Man Who Got Arrested on Purpose**

**The Story:**
- Got himself ARRESTED to expose police corruption
- Spent 13 days in Lagos cell + Ikoyi Prison
- Documented everything with hidden camera

**The Gonzo Factor:** He didn't just report on police corruption — he LIVED it from inside a jail cell.

### 🇺🇬 Anonymous Ugandan Journalist

## The South African Connection

### Eugène Marais (South Africa, 1871-1936)

**The Original Gonzo**

Way before Thompson, there was Eugène Marais.

**The Story:**
- South African writer who LEFT CIVILIZATION
- Lived for years in the African bush
- Studied ants, termites, wildlife
- Wrote "The Soul of the White Ant" (1925)
- Pioneered observational/nature writing

**Legacy:** Considered one of the first "gonzo" journalists — immersive, first-person, living the story.

---

### Jacques Pauw (South Africa)

**The President's Keeper**

**The Story:**
- Investigative journalist, wrote "The President's Keepers" (2017)
- Exposed massive corruption under President Jacob Zuma
- Book was banned in South Africa (briefly)

**The Price:** Police came for him. Death threats.

## The East African Giants

### 🇰🇪 Binyavanga Wainaina (Kenya, 1971-2019)

**The Kenyan Legend**

**The Story:**
- Founded Kwani? magazine (2003) — Kenya's literary revolution
- Won Caine Prize 2002 for "Discovering Home"
- Wrote "How to Write About Africa" (satirical Granta essay) — MUST READ

**The Gonzo Moments:**
- EU hired him to write about Sudan conflict → they hated his draft → he told them to FROFF and published it himself
- Came out as gay in 2014 Kenya (dangerous)
- Died 2019 in Nairobi

**Legacy:** His satirical essay "How to Write About Africa" is required reading for anyone writing about the continent.

---

### 🇲🇿 Carlos Cardoso (Mozambique, 1956-2000)

**The Murdered Truth**

**The Story:**
- Investigated $1.4 billion bank scandal (Privatization theft)
- Exposed massive corruption

**The End:**
- ASSASSINATED in 2000 in Maputo
- Case never fully solved

---

### 🇦🇴 Rafael Marques de Morais (Angola)

**The Lone Wolf**

**The Story:**
- Runs "Maka Angola" — critical of President Dos Santos regime
- Investigated human rights abuses in diamond regions
- Arrested, beaten, threatened

**Awards:** 2015 Allard Prize for International Integrity

## Themes

1. **Survival Journalism** — Many African journalists don't have a choice. To expose truth, they must become the story.

2. **Cartel Exposure** — Mexico, Brazil, parts of Africa have narco-states. Journalists investigating cartels risk death daily.

3. **Political Corruption** — From Ghana to Angola to South Africa, exposing government corruption is dangerous work.

4. **The Gonzo Pioneers** — Eugène Marais was doing gonzo 50 years before Thompson coined the term.
