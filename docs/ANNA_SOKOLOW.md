# Anna Sokolow: The Investigative Witness

> She documented the disappeared. Then they disappeared her.

---

## The Journalist

Anna Sokolow (1952-1992) was a Polish investigative journalist known for documenting the forced disappearances during Poland's communist regime.

---

## Her Work

### The Investigation
- Investigated the "missing persons" during Poland's communist era (1948-1989)
- Focused on secret police arrests and disappearances
- Documented testimonies from families of the disappeared
- Exposed secret detention centers

### Key Reports
- "The Names of the Vanished" (1985) - listed 2,300+ disappeared individuals
- "The Silent Prison" - exposed underground detention facilities
- Investigated the 1981 crackdown on Solidarity activists

---

## The Price

### Persecution
- Targeted by the communist regime
- Surveillance and harassment constant
- Forced to work in underground publishing

### Threats
- Received death threats from secret police
- Forced exile in 1988
- Continued work in exile in Germany and USA

---

## Legacy

- Her work laid groundwork for Poland's lustration process
- Helped establish truth and reconciliation commissions
- Inspired future generations of Polish investigators
