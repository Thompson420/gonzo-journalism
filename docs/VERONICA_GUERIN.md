# Veronica Guerin: The Irish Legend

> She was murdered for doing her job. Her death changed Ireland forever.

---

## The Journalist

Veronica Guerin (1960-1996) was an Irish investigative journalist who was assassinated in 1996. Her murder shocked Ireland and led to major changes in how the country tackled organized crime.

---

## Her Story

### Early Career
- Started as a journalist in the 1980s
- Worked for the Sunday Independent
- Focused on covering crime and corruption

### What She Exposed
- Drug trafficking in Ireland
- Gangland criminals
- The connection between organized crime and politics
- Named names that others were afraid to mention

### Her Persistence
- Received numerous death threats
- Police warned her multiple times that her life was in danger
- She continued anyway
- "They can kill me, but they can't kill the truth"

---

## The Assassination

### June 26, 1996
- Shot while driving her car
- On her way to court to give evidence
- Died at the scene

### The Aftermath
- Shocked Ireland
- Led to the establishment of the Criminal Assets Bureau (1996)
- Her death is considered a turning point in Ireland's fight against organized crime

---

## Legacy

### Changes She Inspired
- Criminal Assets Bureau (CAB) established
- New laws on journalist protection
- Greater protection for witnesses

### Awards
- Named "Irish Person of the Year" in 1996
- Memorial statue erected in Dublin
