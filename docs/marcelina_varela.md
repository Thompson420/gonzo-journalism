## Detailed Biography
Marcelina "Marca" Varela operated primarily in Córdoba province during Argentina's military dictatorship era (1976-1983). She worked under various pseudonyms to avoid detection, including "La Pata" (The Paw).

### Key Investigations
- Exposed military censorship practices in Córdoba province
- Documented disappearances and torture by security forces
- Published underground newsletter "La Voz de los Desaparecidos" (The Voice of the Disappeared)
- Collaborated with international human rights organizations

### Personal Risk
- Forced into exile multiple times during her career
- Worked under constant surveillance and threat of arrest
- Maintained network of informants within military and police ranks
- Used photography to document censorship practices (now in human rights archives)

### Legacy
- Considered one of Argentina's pioneer investigative journalists
- Inspired generations of Latin American investigative journalists
- Her methods influenced later gonzo-style reporting in Latin America
- Oral histories preserved by Argentine human rights organizations
