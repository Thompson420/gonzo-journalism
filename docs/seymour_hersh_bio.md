# Seymour Hersh - The Investigative Legend

## Full Biography

**Seymour David Hersh** was born on April 8, 1937, in Chicago, Illinois. He is an American investigative journalist and author who has won the Pulitzer Prize twice and is considered one of the most important investigative journalists of the 20th century.

## Early Life

- Born in Chicago to Jewish immigrants
- University of Chicago (dropped out)
- Worked for Chicago City News Bureau

## Career

### Early Work
- Reporter for the city desk (City News Bureau)
- Covered police beat

### AP and Beyond
- Associated Press (1960s)
- Dispatched to Vietnam
- Joined The New Yorker (1971)

## Landmark Investigations

### 1. My Lai Massacre (1969)
**The Story:**
- American soldiers killed 500+ Vietnamese civilians
- Hersh obtained first interview with surviving soldiers
- Won **1970 Pulitzer Prize**

### 2. Abu Ghraib (2004)
**The Story:**
- Prisoner abuse at Abu Ghraib prison in Iraq
- Photos shocked the world
- Led to Congressional hearings

### 3. Syrian Chemical Weapons (2013)
**The Story:**
- Exposed Obama's administration lying about sarin gas attack
- Contradicted official narrative
- Won **2015 Pulitzer Prize**

### 4. Biden Ukraine (2020s)
**The Story:**
- Exposed Biden family's business deals in Ukraine
- Challenged media narratives
- Political firestorm

## Methodology

- Cultivates confidential sources
- First-person immersive reporting
- Long-form narrative
- "He said, she said" rarely used

## Awards & Recognition

- **1970 Pulitzer Prize** (National Reporting)
- **1975 George Polk Award**
- **2005 Pulitzer Prize** (Feature Writing)
- **2019 George Polk Award**
