# Hunter S. Thompson - The Father of Gonzo Journalism

## Full Biography

**Hunter Stockton Thompson** (born November 18, 1937 - died February 20, 2005) was an American journalist and author who is credited with creating the style of journalism known as "gonzo journalism."

## Early Life

- Born in Louisville, Kentucky
- Father: Jack Thompson (sheriff's deputy)
- Mother: Virginia (civil rights activist)
- Youngest of three children

### Formative Years
- Dropped out of high school at age 15
- Worked as a journalist for local papers
- Brief stint in US Air Force (discharged for "inadaptability")

## Career

### Early Journalism (1950s-1960s)
- Wrote for newspapers in Puerto Rico, Brazil, and the American South
- Published in prestigious outlets like **The New York Times** and **The Nation**

### The Breakthrough: "The Kentucky Derby Is Decadent and Depraved" (1970)
- His first major gonzo piece
- Published in **Scanlan's Monthly**
- Sent to cover the Kentucky Derby as a sports writer
- Instead wrote about the characters and culture
- Defined the gonzo approach

## The Gonzo Manifesto

### Key Principles

1. **First-Person Immersion**
   - Journalist becomes the story
   - No objective observer

2. **No Neutrality**
   - Embrace subjectivity
   - Take a side

3. **Literary Techniques**
   - Fiction writing in nonfiction
   - Dramatic, vivid prose

4. **Participant Observer**
   - Embed with subjects
   - Experience what you report

5. **Psychedelic Realism**
   - Heightened, surreal descriptions
   - "Fear and Loathing" style

## Landmark Works

### Hell's Angels (1966)
- Embedded with the motorcycle gang for 18 months
- First book
- Gang sued for defamation
- Became famous

### Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas (1971)
- Defining gonzo text
- Published in **Rolling Stone**
- Raoul Duke persona
- "A savage journey to the heart of the American Dream"

### Other Key Works
- "Fear and Loathing on the Campaign Trail '72" (1973)
- "The Great Shark Hunt" (1979)
- "The Rum Diary" (1998)
- "Kingdom of Fear" (2003)

## Political Campaigns

### 1970: Sheriff of Pitkin County
- Ran as independent
- Used gonzo campaign tactics
- Lost by 13 votes

### 1972: Presidential Campaign
- Covered for Rolling Stone
- "Fear and Loathing on the Campaign Trail"
- Documented Nixon campaign

## Personal Life

### The Wild Years
- Famous for wild parties
- Cannon-firing incidents
- Drug use
- Eccentric behavior

### The Compound
- "Owl Farm" in Colorado
- Built a 153-foot cannon
- Ran for sheriff

### Family
- Married three times
- Son: Juan Thompson (journalist)
- Committed suicide: February 20, 2005

## Death

### Final Act
- Shot himself at Owl Farm
- Left note: "No More Games. No More Bombs. No More Walking"
- Final request: Firecracker cannon salute

### Ashes
- Blown from cannon atop 153-foot tower
- "Goodnight, sweet prince"

- Created an entire journalism genre

- Politics: Inspired campaign journalism

## Methodology

### The Gonzo Process
1. Find a story
2. Immerse yourself completely
3. Take copious notes
4. Write in first person
5. Let the story write itself

### What Made Him Different
- Rejected objectivity
- Made himself the subject
- Used fiction techniques
- Created a persona
